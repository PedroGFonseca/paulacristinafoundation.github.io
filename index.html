Mission

To create new generations of female secondary-school graduates that positively empower their local communities

Vision

Live in a world where every girl who wants to study can do so safely and effectively

Motto

Happy, safe and empowered girls

The Paula Cristina Foundation provides continuous education support to girls in Kenya, focusing on long-term scholarships that guarantee support through graduation or adulthood. We believe in empowering local communities through education, with a commitment to careful geographic expansion as relationships develop.

What We Do

Three core programs supporting girls' education

Scholarships

Multi-year scholarships covering the gap between what families can provide and what students need to stay in school. We offer regular scholarships (fees, materials, transport, medical insurance), enhanced scholarships (including food and essentials), and special scholarships custom-designed for students with unique needs.

School Support Programs

Collective support for partner schools including vaccination programs, hygiene initiatives, and reusable supplies. Building stronger educational infrastructure for entire communities through collaborative programs.

Emergency Relief

Temporary support for students and schools during emergencies. Ensuring continuity of education even in challenging circumstances, helping students stay on track toward graduation.

Objectives & Policy Highlights

Foundation Objectives: The Foundation supports access to pre-primary, primary, secondary, technical and higher education, with a particular focus on girls. We operate primarily in Kenya and may expand to other countries as partnerships develop. We also provide health services, hygiene support, safe housing, and other essential services that enable students to access education.

Main Activities: Multi-year scholarships covering educational expenses (fees, materials, transport, boarding); health and hygiene support (sanitary products, vaccinations, reproductive health education); basic necessities for educational access (food, housing, medical insurance); support for local implementing partners; and emergency relief to ensure educational continuity during crises.

Fundraising & Asset Management: Funds are raised through donations and grants. At least 90% of our activities serve public benefit. The Foundation is non-profit. In case of dissolution, assets will be directed to a similar educational ANBI organization.
